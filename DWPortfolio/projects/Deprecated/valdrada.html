City of Valdrada

(2022)

Roles: Lead Developer

Developed as part of an Immersive Media assignment, this VR experience was a collaborative effort by a team of three, inspired by Italo Calvino's *Invisible Cities*. Our group chose the ethereal city of Valdrada, known for its reflection in the waters below. Over the course of a week and a half, we transformed this concept into a 3D scene.

  • Sketched out the basic layout for the map and decided on the necessary assets.
  • Created seven distinct building types using the walls and verandas provided by teammates.
  • Navigated challenges of integrating 2D assets into a 3D environment, addressing lighting and render order issues.
  • Used Substance Designer to create textures for dirt, water, and sand.
  • Utilized Blender and Shader Graph to create grass and water.
  • Performed minor Photoshop work for particle effects.

Things I Learned

  • Planned out the needed assets for the environement
  • Unity shader graph that go into effects such as vertex displacement, fresnel, camera depth, etc
  • Gained familiarity with Substance Designer and its node-based textures
  • Crafting environments with atmosphere

Highlight of the Project

Working on this project was a chance for me to really dive into the visual side of game development, something I hadn't focused on much before. It turned out to be a great learning experience, especially in creating and using assets efficiently. I'm particularly proud of how I managed to use a few asset variations to build the entire set of buildings in the scene. It was like solving a puzzle, figuring out how to get the most out of each piece to create a diverse and engaging environment. What stands out to me is how these choices in asset use really brought the whole scene together. It looked good, and it felt right â€“ it was just how I envisioned it. This project wasn't just about making things look pretty; it was a practical lesson in making the most of what you have, and I think that's something that will stick with me in my future projects.
